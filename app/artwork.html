    PUI PUI MOLCAR

    loremn ipsum
    Par MISATO Tomoki
    12 mars 2022
    En savoir plus

    En savoir plus

    Born in Tokyo in 1992. Graduated from Musashino Art University, Faculty of Art and Design, Department of Visual Communication Design in 2016, and completed the Department of Animation, Graduate School of Imaging, Tokyo University of the Arts in 2018. The work "My Little Goat" announced as a graduate school completion work won the "SHORT SHORTS FILM FESTIVAL & ASIA 2019" Japan Division Excellence Award, the "Paris International Fantastic Film Festival (PIFFF)" Grand Prix, Argentina, Morocco, Germany, Received numerous awards at international film and animation festivals such as Poland. It is attracting attention as a newcomer to puppet anime expectations. For commercial works, directed the ending animation of "Kedama no Gonjiro" (2019 / TV Tokyo). This work "Pui Pui Molcar" will be the first TV anime series director work.

    A TV series of 2:40-minute animated shorts filmed in stop-motion. It takes place in a world made of wool felt where “Molcars,” guinea pig cars, live together with humans. Although it is a stop-motion animation, the camera moves freely in all directions, and intense action scenes with car chases, crashes, and explosions are depicted with a rich sense of realism. The Molcars are voiced by actual guinea pigs, and live-action humans appear in pixilation. The story unfolds in the bizarre situation of humans riding guinea pigs (Molcars), but verbal explanations such as narration and subtitles are removed from the work as much as possible, leaving the viewer to use their imagination and enjoy the film beyond language. The uproar caused by the Molcars is not only adorable but is also intended to send a message with a social satire.
